Senior Modeler

San Francisco Area (Newark, CA, United States) Position: 11005
Senior CAT Modeler
Responsibilities include research related to the performance of buildings in severe wind (hurricane/tornado) catastrophe computer-based risk models. Development of vulnerability functions for application in catastrophe computer based risk models for US and international countries. Research into the physical nature of wind phenomena and development of wind vulnerability functions for property and business interruption coverages. Review and interpretation of past event data will play a critical role in that process. Need to understand and develop state of the art practices in the field wind engineering and meteorology. Technical support both internally and for clients will be required. The position will require independent work, as well as oversight of the work of others.
 
The Model Development group focuses on developing industry leading stochastic risk models for the quantification and management of catastrophe risk. From regional based hurricane and earthquake models to pandemic disease and terrorism risk, the industry and our 400+ clients recognize RMS as the largest developer of catastrophe models in the risk market.  As RMS continues to grow as a company, the Model Development group will continue to expand its breadth and fields of expertise through the additions of globally correlated natural catastrophe models while simultaneously moving into adjacent markets such as the life and mortality modeling industry. RMS continues to drive industry innovation with the enhancement and expansion of our leading models.
 
Skills and Experience Required:
To be successful in the role of Senior Modeler, you must be/have:
  • M.S. or Ph.D. in civil/structural engineering or related fields.
  • 4-7 years of experience developing natural hazard models.
  •  In-depth knowledge of wind engineering/meteorology. Knowledge of building design and codes for wind a plus.
  •  Lead complex projects independently with minimum direction.
  • Good understanding of the model loss results is essential for this position.
  • Project management experience, ability to work on multiple projects, and the ability to work well in a team environment is essential.
  • Excellent verbal and communication scheme required.
  • High level of proficiency in data analysis and data manipulation software tools (SQL, MS Access, etc.), and a working knowledge in GIS tools (MapInfo, ArcInfo, etc.) a plus, but not necessary.
  • Good programming skill in any high-level language required.
