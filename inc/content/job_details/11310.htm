Senior Director, Total Rewards and HRMS

San Francisco Area (Newark, CA, United States) Position: 11310
The Company
 
RMS is the world’s leading provider of products and services for the quantification and management of catastrophe risk associated with earthquake, hurricane, windstorm, flood, pandemic disease and terrorism peril events. RMS enables clients to run and gain a deeper understanding of their financial risk through use of our cutting edge suite of enterprise software applications. Currently more than 400 insurers, reinsurers, brokers and other financial institutions around the world utilize RMS models and applications to quantify, manage and transfer risk.
 
RMS has experienced significant consistent growth over the last 20 years, with double-digit growth rates in net income and revenue each year, above average profit margins that create a stable base for investment and returns, and market share increase that is reflective of a true market leader, with above 50% of the addressable market.  Even with its tremendous growth, RMS has retained its entrepreneurial culture and single-minded focus on developing industry leading models and analytic applications to help clients make the best decisions in mitigating financial risk. Today, RMS is the industry leader in catastrophic risk modeling as it continues to build a cutting edge, high-performance Enterprise Platform to set the standard in risk management.
 
Role Summary
 
The Senior Director, Total Rewards and HRMS role spans across RMS globally, responsible for leading the design, development, implementation, administration and communication of innovative Compensation, Benefits and Human Resources Management Systems (HRMS) strategies, processes, policies and procedures necessary to drive our human capital strategy and thus, to contribute to optimum business results.  This position plays a highly visible leadership role, critical to the growth and evolution of RMS.  It is responsible for working as a partner to and with our RMS Operating Committee, the SVP HR and other stakeholders to build, influence and drive Total Rewards and HRMS strategies and programs linked to the achievement of company goals, strategies and plans.
 
The Opportunity

The Senior Director of Total rewards and HRMS is a unique opportunity with an innovative, market leader with ambitions and plans for aggressive growth and organizational development.  The position was designed for a creative, seasoned leader, who will have a highly visible role and the autonomy to design and implement their vision, ideas, plans and strategy in collaboration with other senior HR professionals and business leaders.    More specifically, the successful leader will:
  1. Lead the design and development of RMS’s global Compensation and Benefits strategy in support of corporate goals and strategies.
  2. Develop strong business partnerships with leaders and hiring managers at all levels across the company to proactively understand their needs, identify market trends and analyze competitive data with respect to attracting, retaining and rewarding a high quality, diverse workforce.
  3. Lead the design, implementation and management of robust reporting and analytics that drive business and human capital intelligence efforts across the global enterprise.
  4. Lead the implementation, training/communication, and rollout of WorkDay HRMS.
  5. Ensure the efficient and accurate administration of all human capital processes, linkages with other organizations and reporting at BU, Executive and Board levels.
  6. Lead the design and redesign of short and long-term incentive plans that are competitive, cost effective and consistent with company goals and philosophy.
  7. Build, mentor, guide and coach a team to deliver results and increase impact and satisfaction.
  8. Be a contributing member of the HR Leadership Team.  In addition to leading the Team on issues relating to this position’s functional responsibilities, this role will also partner with HR peers in all aspects of human capital work including (but not limited to) employee engagement, talent acquisition, high-potential development, workforce talent assessment, organization development and performance management.
  9. Direct compensation and benefits activities in response to federal; state legislative, regulatory acts, reporting and disclosure requirements.
Mission Critical Objective

During the first 3 – 6 months, the new Director of Total Rewards and HRMS will be expected to accomplish the following critical objectives:
  • Work closely with HR and business leaders in developing a global compensation approach and key inputs into the FY12 Operating budget that includes consideration of geographic labor markets, skill sets, performance, career-pathing and internal/external equity.
  • Lead the global implementation and rollout of Workday Human Capital Management System.
  • Prepare for and execute a focal Compensation Review.

  • Strong leader who can build consensus across multiple constituencies and lead cross-functional teams to deliver measurable results.
  • Ability to establish trust and influence in both client and HR relationships.
  • Process oriented with a strong attention to detail, willingness and ability to be hands-on and possesses excellent analytical and problem solving skills.
  • Solid teamwork and influencing/collaboration skills, prefers to work as a team player, is cooperative, collaborative and easily gain support of peers in a variety of environments, styles and cultures.
  • Excellent verbal and written communication skills: articulate, clear thinker, writer and presenter and able to provide effective communication to all levels of an organization.
  • Strategic thinker with proven aptitude in executing deliverables over a multi-year time frame.
  • Thrives on dealing with ambiguity and change and is very comfortable creating clarity, focus and action.
