Compensation Program Manager

San Francisco Area (Newark, CA, United States) Position: 11274
JOB TITLE: Compensation Program Manager
Job Type: Contractor (may go perm)
 
JOB SUMMARY
Working as a senior level individual contributor, consults with business leaders and HR business partners to develop, implement, and administer compensation plans that meet business objectives and align with broader business strategies.  Ensures programs are in compliance with wage and hour laws, minimum wage, FLSA, and state and federal compensation regulations, as well as corporate policies and procedures. 
 
ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES include the following. Other duties may be assigned.
  • Plan, execute, and lead compensation projects requiring analysis of factors that cross organizational lines and impact multiple organizational units
  • Formulate and implement plans, lead teams to accomplish critical tasks on a defined schedule.
  • Establish a trusted advisor role with the business and serve as a subject matter expert in competitive practices in relation to incentive and total compensation.
  • Provide advice, direction, and guidance on broadly defined compensation matters. 
  • Analyze positions to determine exempt/non-exempt status, compare internal and external positions, and determine competitive pay levels
  • Design, develop, and implement new compensation programs to ensure that internal and external equity is maintained
  • Ensure that job descriptions on file are current, complete, and accurate
  • Administer compensation programs to ensure they are in compliance with wage and hour laws, minimum wage, FLSA, and other state and federal compensation regulations
  • Analyze salary survey data and develop recommendations to ensure competitive salary structures and variable compensation
  • Recommend, conduct, and participate in salary surveys in order to maintain competitive compensation levels in the organization
  • Participate in company implementation of Workday, leading the compensation component
  • Leads and participates in teams chartered with the continuous improvement of compensation best practices and other HR initiatives
QUALIFICATIONS  
  • Bachelor’s degree in Human Resources, Business, Finance, or other related area is required.
  • 5-7 years experience in local and international compensation to include development of job descriptions, survey participation, market based pricing, incentive design, and pay recommendations    
  • Must possess knowledge of Fair Labor Standards Act
  • Solid organizational and multi-tasking skills to include effective time management in all key responsibilities of the job
  • Strong analytical and problem solving skills, and attention to detail 
  • Demonstrated experience building strong client relationships using solid interpersonal and collaborative skills
  • Demonstrated consultative skills and experience influencing business decisions
  • Effective verbal, written, and presentation skills with demonstrated ability to present complex ideas to senior management.
  • Ability to work independently and within a team and ability to handle multiple projects and priorities
  • Ability to interface effectively with senior leadership team
  • Possesses international compensation experience
  • Proficiency in the use of PC software tools for analysis, audits, reports, and presentations including strong Excel (pivot tables and v-look up)

PREFERRED
  • CCP (Certified Compensation Professional) or progress toward certification is a plus
