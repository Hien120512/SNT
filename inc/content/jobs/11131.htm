Risk Analyst

New York City Metro (Hackensack, NJ, United States) Position: 11131
Do you know that there is a 2.4% chance that a hurricane will cause $75 billion of insured losses this year?   And that there is a 1.0% chance that an earthquake will cause $50 billion of insured loss?  We do.  We are RMS, and we build the simulation models that allow insurers and investors to figure out the odds of losing money due to catastrophes:  natural catastrophes (hurricane, earthquake, flood), terrorism, pandemic, and longevity. 
 
We are one of the most exciting and technologically sophisticated firms you never heard of (unless you are in the insurance or reinsurance market, in which case you’re sure to have heard of us).    To build our models, we bring together experts from many disciplines:  meteorologists, seismologists, epidemiologists, terrorism experts, structural engineers, financial engineers.  On the technology side, we are dealing with massive simulations – and are therefore on the cutting edge of high performance grid computing.
 
Our growth for the past 20 years has been mainly in licensing our models to the P&C insurance and reinsurance industry through a software platform called RiskLink.  Two years ago, we launched a Capital Markets business unit to provide software and service to a growing community of investors in catastrophe-linked securities.    We are now 30 strong, and are looking for analysts to join our team.

The Role
We are looking for people with strong quantitative skills to join our Risk Analyst program.  You will come in comfortable with math and probability, and we will help you quickly gain the knowledge to be an expert in the insurance-linked securities market.    Your first two years will involve:
 
  • Two months of intensive training on the market we operate in, and the catastrophe models we build
  • On-the-job training for modeling and analyzing insurance-linked securities
  • Designing triggers for catastrophe bonds
  • Working on analytical projects to support our portfolio management software product
  • Support discussions with Rating Agencies and cat bond investors
 
 
After that, you will have the opportunity to specialize in one of several roles:
  • Transaction advisory  -- e.g., becoming a ‘deal lead’ for cat bonds
  • Product management – e.g., designing new features for our software products
  • Account management – e.g., managing the interactions with our various clients
 
 
Key Requirements
  • Quantitative training (e.g., major in math, engineering, econometrics, physics)
  • Demonstrated leadership
  • Track record of ‘getting things done’
  • Ability to work under time pressure
