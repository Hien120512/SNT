Marketing Assistant

San Francisco Area (Newark, CA, United States) Position: 11257
Request for Corporate Marketing Assistant FY11 Q2-3
Proposal
Corporate marketing requires a part-time marketing assistant (20 hours/week) skilled in graphics design from February–May of 2011. The start of 2011 marks the onset of marketing activities for the Client Conference and version 11.0 launch, as well as the second phase of the RMS website redesign.
 
Solution
Seeking a part-time marketing assistant skilled in graphics design, Adobe Creative Suite software, and Microsoft Excel and PowerPoint applications will provide essential graphics support during this critical time period. While a large portion of the assistant’s time will be allocated to graphics responsibilities, we will also allocate a portion to administrative tasks. This will allow team members to focus on higher-value tasks including developing the new website, ensuring a high level of quality and professionalism in all aspects of the conference, and providing support in graphics and collateral development.
The marketing assistance will provide essential client conference support, both for graphics and administrative tasks. The assistant will aid in proofing and polishing presentation and banner layouts and graphics, and will help take on basic tasks such as data entry, allowing  team members to focus on other key areas of planning such as enhancing the demo room experience, streamlining dry run processes, and creating a more effective, compelling conference experience.
Post-conference, the assistant can help edit videos and presentations, allowing clients to access these presentations while the conference agenda is still fresh in their minds. And, by helping to consolidate survey data, the assistant will help the planning team to share conference satisfaction results internally in a timely manner.
 
 
Assistant Responsibilities and Requirements
Graphics
The assistant will foremost have the graphics skills and training required to provide graphic design support for major client events, publications, Web updates, presentations, and other internal graphic needs. Examples include:

§  Review and polish  Client Conference presentations, banners, and posters

§  Handle all graphics requests from other departments, working with Word, Outlook, Excel and PowerPoint, etc.

Administrative

§  Data entry
